About

Hi, I’m Nhung - you can also call me Velvetta, or Vel. Welcome to my page. I recently completed my PhD in Cyber Security at The University of Queensland, Australia.

My research lies at the intersection of cybersecurity and safety for autonomous and cyber-physical systems. I am fascinated by how cyber attacks can influence the behaviour of autonomous systems and how we can design security mechanisms that not only detect attacks but also enable systems to continue operating safely and reliably under adverse conditions.

My journey into cybersecurity began with an Engineer degree in Information Technology from Hanoi University of Science and Technology. After graduation, I spent ten years at the Ministry of Public Security, Vietnam, where I worked in cybersecurity research and development, enterprise security, incident response, and cybercrime countermeasures. Those years gave me first-hand experience with protecting complex systems in real-world environments and inspired me to pursue a PhD to investigate these challenges from a research perspective.

Today, my research spans multiple aspects of autonomous vehicle cybersecurity. I investigate cyber attacks targeting both communication protocols and perception systems, including CAN and UDS protocol attacks, camera-based perception attacks, and their impact on vehicle behaviour and safety. On the defence side, I develop security mechanisms such as protocol authentication, access control, and intrusion detection systems using rule-based, statistical, and machine learning approaches. These mechanisms are integrated with safety-aware response frameworks and vehicle control strategies, allowing autonomous systems to detect attacks, respond appropriately, and continue their missions safely whenever possible.

Beyond attack detection and response, I enjoy building structured models that explain complex cybersecurity problems. My research includes graph-based attack–defence modelling, stochastic Petri nets and state-based response frameworks. I have also contributed to the development of structured knowledge bases that organise attack and defence techniques for autonomous and unmanned systems, supporting systematic security analysis and resilient system design.

Research interests: Autonomous Vehicle Security, Cyber-Physical Systems, Attack Modelling, Simulation, Safety Analysis, Intrusion Detection, Resilience Engineering

Research

AV-ODF (Autonomous Vehicle Offence–Defence Framework)

A knowledge-based framework for analysing cyber attacks and attacker behaviour in autonomous vehicles across multiple phases. It organises attack techniques in a structured way and maps them to mitigation strategies, helping to understand how threats evolve and propagate in vehicle systems.

The framework also connects attack techniques to cyber resilience strategies based on NIST SP 800-160, and to system-level security controls based on NIST SP 800-53, providing a clear link between attacks, defences, and system design.

Project Page | Source Code

UV-ODF (Unmanned Vehicle Threat Matrix)

An extension of the AV framework to unmanned ground vehicles, including UGVs, UAVs, USVs. This project focuses on mapping threats and defensive strategies for military and robotic vehicle systems.

Project Page | Source Code

CARLASec

A simulation framework built on CARLA to study the safety impact of cyber attacks. It allows testing different attack scenarios, such as CAN and UDS-based attacks, and observing how they affect vehicle behaviour in real time.

Installation Guide | Source Code

AVHARM

A hierarchical modelling approach for analysing security risks in vehicle systems. It represents the system using structured models and evaluates how vulnerabilities can propagate and lead to system compromise and safety impact.

The approach combines attack graphs, attack fault trees, and attack countermeasure trees. Vehicle systems are modelled in a structured, JSON-based format, enabling systematic analysis of attack paths, risks, and defence effectiveness.
