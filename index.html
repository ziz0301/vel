About

Hi, I’m Nhung - you can also call me Velvetta, or Vel. Welcome to my page. I recently completed my PhD in Cyber Security at The University of Queensland, Australia.

My research focuses on cybersecurity and safety in autonomous and cyber-physical systems. I am particularly interested in understanding how attacks affect system behaviour, and how we can design defences that reduce their impact.

I enjoy building structured models to describe complex systems. These models help explain how vulnerabilities propagate and how different defence strategies can improve resilience.

Research interests: Autonomous Vehicle Security, Cyber-Physical Systems, Attack Modelling, Simulation, Safety Analysis, Intrusion Detection, Resilience Engineering

Research

AV-ODF (Autonomous Vehicle Offence–Defence Framework)

A knowledge-based framework for analysing cyber attacks and attacker behaviour in autonomous vehicles across multiple phases. It organises attack techniques in a structured way and maps them to mitigation strategies, helping to understand how threats evolve and propagate in vehicle systems.

The framework also connects attack techniques to cyber resilience strategies based on NIST SP 800-160, and to system-level security controls based on NIST SP 800-53, providing a clear link between attacks, defences, and system design.

Project Page | Source Code

UV-ODF (Unmanned Vehicle Threat Matrix)

An extension of the AV framework to unmanned ground vehicles, including UGVs, UAVs, USVs. This project focuses on mapping threats and defensive strategies for military and robotic vehicle systems.

Project Page | Source Code

CARLASec

A simulation framework built on CARLA to study the safety impact of cyber attacks. It allows testing different attack scenarios, such as CAN and UDS-based attacks, and observing how they affect vehicle behaviour in real time.

Installation Guide | Source Code

AVHARM

A hierarchical modelling approach for analysing security risks in vehicle systems. It represents the system using structured models and evaluates how vulnerabilities can propagate and lead to system compromise and safety impact.

The approach combines attack graphs, attack fault trees, and attack countermeasure trees. Vehicle systems are modelled in a structured, JSON-based format, enabling systematic analysis of attack paths, risks, and defence effectiveness.
